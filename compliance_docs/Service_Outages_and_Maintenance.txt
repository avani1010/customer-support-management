DEPARTMENT: Service Outages and Maintenance
ROUTING SCOPE AND PRIORITY GUIDANCE
=====================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The Service Outages and Maintenance department handles incidents and
communications related to infrastructure-level service disruptions affecting
multiple users or systems simultaneously, as well as planned maintenance
windows and digital platform overhaul requests.

In the training data, this queue is heavily weighted toward HIGH priority
tickets (approx. 70% high). It receives tickets about: system-wide outages
affecting multiple products, digital marketing platform disruptions caused by
infrastructure changes, service interruptions impacting marketing agency tools,
data analytics platform failures at scale, and planned infrastructure updates.
Unlike IT Support — which handles technical problems affecting individual users
or teams — this department deals with systemic issues where the scope of impact
goes beyond a single user.

Key ticket types: Incident (service outages, system failures), Change (planned
infrastructure updates, digital strategy overhauls), Request (information on
upcoming maintenance, feature roadmap), Problem (recurring multi-user platform
degradation).

COMMON TAGS IN THIS QUEUE
--------------------------
Tech Support, IT, Outage, Disruption, Performance, Network, Feedback,
Documentation, Maintenance, Recovery, Security, Technical, Bug, Feature,
Resolution.

REPRESENTATIVE EXAMPLES
-----------------------
  - "Experiencing Service Outage Due to Potential Infrastructure Overload:
    There is a service outage possibly caused by infrastructure overload. The
    affected servers were restarted by the team, but the problem persists." [high|Incident]

  - "Urgent Improvement of Digital Strategy: Requesting improvements to our
    digital strategies as a result of persistent service outages impacting our
    core marketing tools. These outages have caused substantial disruptions
    to our operations, leading to missed deadlines and lost opportunities." [high|Change]

  - "Service Interruptions Affecting Marketing Agency Systems: The marketing
    agency is facing service interruptions impacting several digital tools and
    hardware. The issue appears to be linked to recent infrastructure updates." [high|Incident]

  - "Trouble with Digital Marketing Campaigns: Our digital marketing campaigns
    have ceased to generate leads and engagement, possibly due to recent changes
    in the platform's algorithm affecting multiple users." [high|Incident]

  - "Enhancement of Data Analytics Update: Updating the data analytics
    infrastructure is anticipated to enhance investment optimization across
    various products. This update is expected to improve performance broadly." [high|Change]

  - "Sudden Disruption of Multiple Product Connections: An update to the
    network configuration led to routing conflicts, impacting Switch, Android,
    and other devices across the organisation." [medium|Incident]

  - "Problem with Digital Platforms: Currently encountering service disruptions
    on essential digital platforms. Recent configuration changes could be the
    cause. Restart attempts have not resolved the issue." [high|Problem]

  - "Issue Reported Concerning the Investments Optimization Tool: I am
    addressing a problem with the investments optimization tool affecting
    multiple users on the platform. The tool is experiencing intermittent
    failures." [low|Incident]

PRIORITY GUIDANCE FOR THIS QUEUE
---------------------------------
HIGH: A business-critical system is completely unavailable with no workaround;
multiple users or the entire organisation is blocked from working; service
outage is actively causing revenue loss or operational disruption; infrastructure
update is urgently required due to security or compliance risk; outage is
impacting marketing tools during an active campaign launch.

MEDIUM: Service is degraded but partially functional for some users; a planned
maintenance event needs coordination; disruption is limited to specific regions
or product lines; an infrastructure change is scheduled and needs communication.

LOW: Single-user impact that might indicate a wider issue (monitor but do not
escalate yet); post-incident review request once service is fully restored;
informational request about upcoming maintenance windows; minor degradation
with a workaround in place.

ROUTING DECISION GUIDANCE
--------------------------
Route to Service Outages and Maintenance when: multiple users are affected by
the same issue simultaneously; the issue appears to be platform- or
infrastructure-level rather than user-specific; a planned maintenance event
needs to be communicated or coordinated; or a digital strategy overhaul is
required in response to a systemic service failure.

If only one user is affected and the cause is likely user-specific (e.g., their
VPN config, their device, their account), route to IT Support instead.
