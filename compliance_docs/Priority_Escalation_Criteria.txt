PRIORITY ESCALATION CRITERIA
Universal routing guidance — applies across all departments
============================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Priority is determined by business impact, urgency, and scope of effect —
not by department. This document defines HIGH, MEDIUM, and LOW priority
criteria that apply regardless of which department a ticket is routed to.

Training data distribution: HIGH (38%), MEDIUM (41%), LOW (20%).
The classifier must distinguish these from language patterns alone.
This document provides grounding for when to confirm, escalate, or
de-escalate a priority prediction.

PRIORITY: HIGH
--------------
Route as HIGH priority when any of the following apply:
- Multiple users or the entire organisation is blocked from working
- Revenue is actively being lost (checkout down, payments failing live,
  campaigns completely non-functional)
- A financial transaction has already occurred incorrectly (fraud, double
  charge, unauthorised billing) and money needs to be recovered
- A business-critical system is completely unavailable with no workaround
- An employee cannot start work on day one due to missing access or equipment
- A security incident is in progress (malware, ransomware, confirmed breach,
  unauthorized access to medical or financial data)
- A deal is closing imminently and the customer is at risk of going to a
  competitor; urgent brand strategy request with imminent deadline
- There is regulatory, legal, or data safety exposure requiring immediate action
- A return window is about to expire and the customer has not received a label
- Service outage affecting multiple products or users simultaneously with no
  workaround

Language signals that strongly predict HIGH priority:
"urgent", "critical", "all users", "entire organisation", "cannot work",
"data breach", "unauthorized access", "system down", "complete failure",
"imminently", "losing clients", "patient care impacted", "revenue at risk",
"no workaround", "immediate action required".

Representative high-priority examples from training data:
  - "Support Request for System Outages: Facing system outages that are
    impacting multiple connected devices during peak usage hours." [IT Support]

  - "Urgent Assistance Required for Medical Data Security Breach: Concerned
    about the recent unauthorized exposure of medical details via unsecured
    equipment. This is affecting all departments." [IT Support]

  - "Experiencing Service Outage Due to Potential Infrastructure Overload:
    There is a service outage possibly caused by infrastructure overload.
    The affected servers were restarted but the problem persists." [Service Outages]

  - "Payment Discrepancy: I am contacting you regarding an unexpected payment
    discrepancy in my recent invoice. The amount charged does not match the
    agreed rate and requires immediate correction." [Billing]

  - "Today's Problem with Data Sync: Faced data synchronization problems during
    the usual update process, affecting all users. After restarting, the issue
    persists and team is blocked." [Customer Service]

PRIORITY: MEDIUM
----------------
Route as MEDIUM when the issue is real and needs resolution within normal SLA
windows but is not causing immediate loss or blocking the customer entirely:
- Single user affected, not the whole organisation
- Service is degraded but partially working
- Analytics tool producing inaccurate results but still functional
- Billing discrepancy exists but no money lost yet; invoice clarification needed
- Escalation request where the customer is unhappy but stable
- Integration failure affecting one workflow with partial workaround
- Pre-sales prospect is actively evaluating but not yet at decision stage
- Return or exchange is in progress but not time-critical
- Performance degradation (slow load times, sluggish responses) without
  complete outage
- Recurring problem that is manageable but needs permanent resolution

Language signals that predict MEDIUM priority:
"intermittent", "slow", "degraded", "discrepancy", "sometimes", "partially",
"one user", "we have a workaround", "need resolution within the week",
"not urgent but important", "sporadic", "inaccurate results".

Representative medium-priority examples from training data:
  - "Problems with Login Post Update: I am facing a login problem since the
    latest update. It might be related to cache. I have a workaround using
    incognito mode but need a permanent fix." [IT Support]

  - "Concern Regarding Monthly Invoices: A billing discrepancy was noticed on
    last month's invoice. This might be caused by a synchronization error." [Billing]

  - "Intermittent Access Problems on SaaS Platform: Facing sporadic connectivity
    difficulties with the cloud-native SaaS system. The platform is partially
    usable." [Customer Service]

  - "Sudden Disruption of Multiple Product Connections: A network configuration
    update led to routing conflicts impacting some but not all devices." [Service Outages]

  - "Request for Support with PostgreSQL Integration: Seeking assistance
    integrating PostgreSQL 13 with data analytics tools for an active project." [Customer Service]

PRIORITY: LOW
-------------
Route as LOW when the ticket is informational, administrative, or the customer
has indicated no urgency:
- General question with no active problem
- Request to update contact or billing details for future use
- Pre-sales inquiry at early exploration stage with no deadline
- Routine status check on an active but non-urgent ticket
- Minor performance issue or UI bug with a workaround
- Post-incident review request once service is fully restored
- Single-user issue that is inconvenient but not blocking work
- Documentation or guidance requests with no stated deadline
- Early-stage brand or marketing strategy inquiries

Language signals that predict LOW priority:
"when you have a chance", "no rush", "just wondering", "for future reference",
"informational", "exploring options", "minor issue", "workaround in place",
"post-incident", "general question", "could you provide information".

Representative low-priority examples from training data:
  - "Query on Integrating Analytics Tools with KNIME for Investment Optimization:
    I am requesting information on analytics tools that can be integrated with
    KNIME to enhance investment optimization workflows." [IT Support]

  - "Inquiry About Detailed Marketing Agency Service Offerings: I am reaching
    out to obtain detailed information about the services provided. No urgency." [Sales]

  - "Update Billing Processes Using Data Analytics: Requesting an update on
    the billing processes, particularly the payment methods utilized. This is
    for future planning." [Billing]

  - "HR Portal Access Problem: One employee is unable to access the HR portal.
    They have a workaround using a colleague's machine in the meantime." [HR]

  - "Problem with Data Synchronization: Encountering occasional data
    synchronization failures across Wave and PrestaShop. Intermittent only." [General Inquiry]
