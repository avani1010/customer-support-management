DEPARTMENT: Returns and Exchanges
ROUTING SCOPE AND PRIORITY GUIDANCE
=====================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The Returns and Exchanges department manages the physical and transactional
lifecycle of product returns, replacements, and exchanges. This includes
initiating return requests, tracking items in transit, processing replacements
for defective or incorrect items, confirming refund timelines post-return, and
resolving disputes where a return was submitted but acknowledgement has not
been received.

Important nuance from training data: this queue receives a broader range of
tickets than the name suggests. It handles product-related performance
complaints, integration failures within project or data management tools,
digital campaign performance issues, and security incident reports — all
framed around a specific product or deliverable that is not meeting
expectations. The connecting thread is that the customer received something
(a product, a service, a feature) that is not working as expected and they
want it corrected or replaced.

Key ticket types: Incident (product malfunctions, synchronization failures,
security breaches affecting a product), Problem (data analytics failures,
investment calculation errors, integration breakdowns), Request (information
on product features, integration options), Change (marketing plan updates).

COMMON TAGS IN THIS QUEUE
--------------------------
Tech Support, Feedback, Performance, IT, Documentation, Bug, Security,
Feature, Sales, Product, Technical, Guidance, Marketing, Resolution, Disruption.

REPRESENTATIVE EXAMPLES
-----------------------
  - "Incomplete Product Bundle Received From Your Company: I recently found
    that my product bundle was incomplete upon opening. Despite attempts to
    resolve, the missing items have not been addressed." [medium|Incident]

  - "Project Management System Data Synchronization Problem: The project
    management SaaS has encountered data synchronization failures across
    various integrations, potentially due to configuration errors." [high|Incident]

  - "Technical Assistance for Financial Data Analytics Tool Malfunction: The
    Financial Data Analytics Tool is not optimizing investment returns due to
    a system glitch and incorrect data inputs." [medium|Incident]

  - "Problem with Investment Calculations: A financial company encountered
    inconsistencies in investment return calculations using H2O.ai SQL Server
    analytics. The issue might be due to data integrity problems." [low|Problem]

  - "Challenge with Campaign Engagement: The agency's recent digital campaign
    has seen lower engagement than anticipated. It might be due to a misaligned
    target audience or ineffective content strategy." [medium|Problem]

  - "Request for Details on Project Management Features: I am inquiring about
    the features of the SaaS project management platform, specifically regarding
    integration options." [medium|Request]

  - "Modify marketing plan: Seeking to update the marketing plan to boost brand
    exposure across product lines." [low|Change]

  - "Problem with Synchronization of Data Across Devices: Customers encounter
    delays in data synchronization even after restarting servers and clearing
    caches." [high|Problem]

PRIORITY GUIDANCE FOR THIS QUEUE
---------------------------------
HIGH: Return window is about to expire and the customer has not received a
shipping label; product failure causing significant business disruption;
data synchronization failure affecting multiple users or systems with no
workaround; confirmed product defect with revenue impact.

MEDIUM: Single product malfunctioning but workaround exists; exchange in
progress but not time-critical; integration failure affecting one workflow;
digital campaign underperforming but not causing immediate financial loss.

LOW: General questions about return processes or product features; minor
product issues with workarounds; marketing plan update requests with no
deadline pressure; informational requests about integration options.

ROUTING DECISION GUIDANCE
--------------------------
Route to Returns and Exchanges when: the customer has a physical product they
want to send back; has already sent one back and is tracking a refund; is
reporting that a delivered product or service did not meet specifications; or
is requesting a replacement or correction of a specific product or deliverable.

Route to Billing and Payments if the conversation is only about a refund amount
or payment dispute with no physical product or deliverable return involved.
Route to IT Support if the issue is a pure technical failure with no product
return dimension.
